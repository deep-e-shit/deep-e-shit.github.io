A Silent Existence: When Your Own Family Feels Like Strangers

By: Deepesh
March 27, 2025 - 22:53

There’s a kind of loneliness that doesn’t come from being alone, but from being surrounded by people who act like you don’t exist. It’s the suffocating silence of being invisible in your own home, where every conversation meant for you is filtered through someone else, where your voice doesn’t matter, and where your existence feels reduced to what you can provide.

My parents don’t speak to me directly anymore. If they need something, they tell my sister, as if I’m just a shadow in the background. When something goes wrong, somehow, it’s always my fault. No matter what I do, no matter how much effort I put into trying to be enough, it never is. I have become nothing more than a wallet, a source of money, a means to an end.

I sit in the same room with them, yet it feels like I’m miles away. They talk around me, not to me. If I disappeared, would they even notice? Or would they just miss the financial security I bring? It’s hard to tell anymore. I have stopped trying to justify their actions, stopped looking for reasons, stopped hoping for change. Maybe this is all I am to them. Maybe this is all I have ever been.

The weight of being unwanted is unbearable. I can’t talk to them about how I feel because they don’t care. I can’t reach out to anyone because there is no one to reach out to. I exist in this void, isolated, unheard, unseen. I want to scream, but what’s the point when no one is listening?

People say that family is everything, but what do you do when your family makes you feel like nothing?
